Experience


Application of optical remote sensing to measure water quality indicators
Research intern at Waterboard Limburg

Carried out a research to find out the advantage of two commercially available optical remote sensing instruments to monitor and measure water quality in rural waterways. A Parrot Sequoia multispectralcamera was used to predict the relationship between turbidity water sample and several band ratios,- band indices- and monochrome bands. A FLIR One Pro thermal camera was also used to investigate how well the thermal sensor is able to measure surface water temperature. Some research results can be found on this page.

Developing a QGIS cloud masking assistance tool
Intern at Eleaf

Satellite imagery mac contains clouds which can disturb the extraction of valuable remote sensing data. Hence, they have to be removed prior to processing. During my internship at Eleaf I developed a cloud masking tool in QGIS with Python and Qt Designer. The tool provide assistance to image analysts to easiy create, mark and save cloud mask layers during pre-processing of satellite images. A documentation of this tool can be found here.

Application of a gamma-ray sensor to estimate potassium availability in soil
Research Intern at Eurofins Agro

Potassium regulates moisture balance and growth of crops, therefore it is a valuable resource to farmers. A study was conducted to investigate the possibilities of a gamma-ray soil scan to estimate potassium availabilty in soil. The gamma-ray scan is able measure various radionuclides (K-40, U-238, Th- 232 and Cs-137). Potassium (K-available) samples were taken from four sandy fields. Linear models were than developed in R Studio to predict the relationship between K-available samples and K-40 scan data.
